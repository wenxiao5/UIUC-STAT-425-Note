  1. Derive a formula relating \(R^2\) and the \(F\) -test for the regression. You can find the formula in the lecture slides. Here you need to derive it.

    \[F=\frac{(RSS_R-RSS_F)}{df_R - df_F} \div \frac{RSS_F}{df_F}\] Using the definition of \(R^2\), we have \[RSS_R = (1-R^2_R) TSS,\,\,\,\,RSS_F = (1-R^2_F) TSS\] So, plugging back to the \(F\) expression we get:

    \[F = \frac{( (1-R^2_R) TSS-(1-R^2_F) TSS)}{df_R - df_F} \div \frac{(1-R^2_F) TSS}{df_F} = \frac{( (1-R^2_R)-(1-R^2_F))}{df_R - df_F} \div \frac{(1-R^2_F) }{df_F} \]

    Observe that here the TSS is the same for the Full and Reduced models, since its calculation only depends on the data not the fitted model.
